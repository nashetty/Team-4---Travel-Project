Australia

Australia is a country and continent located in the southern hemisphere. Its official name is the Commonwealth of Australia and it is made up of six states and two territories. It is the world's sixth-largest country by total area, covering approximately 7.7 million square kilometers.

Australia has a diverse population of around 25 million people, with English being the primary language spoken. However, due to its history of immigration from a variety of countries, there are many different languages heard throughout the country.

Australia is known for its unique wildlife, such as kangaroos, koalas, and wallabies, as well as its iconic landmarks like the Sydney Opera House and Uluru (also known as Ayers Rock).The economy of Australia is highly developed, with a focus on natural resources, such as coal, iron ore, and gold, as well as tourism, agriculture, and education. Australia is also known for its sporting achievements, including in cricket, rugby, swimming, and athletics.

The capital city of Australia is Canberra, and its largest city is Sydney. Other major cities include Melbourne, Brisbane, Perth, Adelaide, and Hobart. Australia has a high standard of living and is considered a desirable place to live and work. Its healthcare system is ranked highly internationally and the country has a strong education system. The Australian government is a federal parliamentary democracy and a member of the United Nations.

Source: Britannica.com

Things to do

Discover the Great Ocean Road

Stretching along the south-eastern coast of Australia, it offers stunning scenery and beautiful landscapes. The Great Ocean Road is an Australian National Heritage listed 240-kilometre stretch of road along the south-eastern coast of Australia between the Victorian cities of Torquay and Allansford.

Explore the Great Barrier Reef

The Great Barrier Reef is the largest living thing on Earth and is visible from outer space! This incredible eco-system is 2,300km long. There are thousands of reefs and hundreds of islands. There are 600 types of hard and soft coral. It is best explored through scuba diving or snorkeling.

Visit Uluru

An important cultural landmark for the indigenous people of Australia, Uluru is a sacred site in the heart of Central Australia. Uluru is probably Australia's most famous natural icon. This huge sandstone monolith stands 1,142 ft above ground (taller than Eiffel Tower 984 ft).

Visit the Sydney Opera House

The iconic structure of Sydney is a must-visit attraction. You can attend a concert or a show or take a guided tour of the building. The Sydney Opera House is a multi-venue performing arts centre in Sydney. It is widely regarded as one of the world's most famous and distinctive buildings and a masterpiece of 20th-century architecture

Safari in the Australian Outback

The vast and beautiful Australian Outback provides a perfect escape to experience the bush, wildlife, and a taste of Australia's unique cultural heritage.
